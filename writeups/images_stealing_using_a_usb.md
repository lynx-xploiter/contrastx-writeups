On 22 December 2024, I was investigating a person who was unofficially making fake visas for people to travel out of the country. I needed proof, but the target kept everything offline – no cloud, no sharing, just local files on his workstation.

Getting physical access was the first challenge. I couldn't just walk in and plug a USB. So I used a simple social engineering trick.

I called his office pretending to be from a local printing company. I said we were running a promotion – free test prints for businesses. All I needed was to let me run a small diagnostic tool from a USB to check color calibration. No installation. Two minutes max. He agreed.

I showed up the next day with the USB in my pocket. The USB looked normal – nothing suspicious. I inserted it, ran the script while pretending to check printer settings, and let it do its work.

The script was designed to:
- Run completely hidden – no command prompt window, no popups, no sound
- Copy only image files – JPG, PNG, GIF, BMP, WEBP, JFIF, ICO, TIFF
- Scan Desktop, Pictures, Documents, Music, Videos, and Downloads
- Keep the original folder structure so files stayed organized
- Save everything directly to a folder on the same USB

While we talked about print quality and paper types, the script ran silently in the background. About two minutes later, I unplugged the USB, said the calibration was successful, and left.

Inside the extracted data, I found:
- Fake visa templates
- Scanned passport copies of clients
- Forged stamp images
- Photoshop project files showing the editing process

That was the proof I needed. The evidence was handed over. The operation was shut down.
